## Abstract

This paper positions the news analysis system **GemminAI** not merely as an information-processing AI, but as a "meteorological observation device for narratives" — the structured stories that shape social reality. In the contemporary information space, narratives emitted by nation-states, markets, and media circulate like wind at speeds that exceed human cognitive capacity, reshaping society at a rate beyond our ability to respond. This research identifies the need for a "measuring instrument" as the first step toward observing this unpredictable storm of information.

GemminAI structures news, market, and historical data, quantifying it as measurable indicators — **CDC (Cross-Domain Correlation: pressure difference)** and **IVD (Information Void Density: visibility degradation)** — to measure the atmospheric state of society. This paper describes the system as a two-stage observation model: a "barometer" and a "weather radar," and further discusses its potential as a future "early warning system (seawall)" against information floods.

## 1. The Problem: Narrative Velocity and the Information Storm

21st-century society is structurally altered by the speed of information. Advances in communication infrastructure have made information transmission between nation-states, markets, and individuals nearly real-time. Yet human decision-making remains constrained by biological limits.

The result is a growing frequency of phenomena such as:

- Flash crashes that strike financial markets like sudden gusts
- Information warfare between nations — deliberate weather manipulation
- Collective emotional spirals on social media (hurricanes)
- Divergence between political narrative and economic reality (high-pressure and low-pressure systems colliding)

The common thread across all these problems is the **absence of an observatory capable of objectively measuring society's "weather."**

Contemporary media conveys events as "stories," but stories are emotional, not quantitative. Society is consequently trapped in the following structure:

```
Event (heat) → Narrative (updraft) → Emotion (cloud formation) → Action (downpour)
```

To address this cascade, rather than attempting to stop the storm, what is needed first is **a weather station — a Stevenson screen — that measures the atmospheric conditions of narrative itself**.

## 2. GemminAI's Design Philosophy

GemminAI is designed as a news analysis system, but its essence is an **atmospheric structure observation device for narratives**. The system is composed of four primary layers:

### 2.1 Core (Structural Layer)

Decomposes news articles into the 24TAG structure, extracting the semantic elements of information. Here, "fact," "claim," "emotion," and "context" are structured. The purpose of this layer is to treat news not as ambiguous text but as **calculable meteorological data (temperature, humidity, wind direction)**.

### 2.2 HRE — Human Relation Engine

A layer that analyzes the dynamics of human relationships underlying the news — the ocean currents and prevailing winds. Elements extracted here include Shared Fear, Trust Break, and Strategic Silence.

### 2.3 TRL / PCC — Physical Constants Layer

A layer that defines the physical conditions of the information environment. Information latency, communication speeds, and geographic barriers are treated as "constants" analogous to gravity or atmospheric pressure.

### 2.4 Oracle (Measurement Layer)

Integrates data from Core, HRE, and TRL to calculate the following indicators:

- **CDC (Cross-Domain Correlation):** The divergence between political narrative and market data — the **"pressure difference"** between distinct domains.
- **IVD (Information Void Density):** The density of information silence — **"dense fog (visibility degradation)"** in the information atmosphere.
- **NP (Narrative Persistence):** The duration of a narrative — the scale of a **"stationary front."**

These are **measured values** indicating the "atmospheric instability" of society.

## 3. The Weather Station (Barometer) Model

The first stage of GemminAI is the "weather station (barometer)."

A weather station measures and describes the current state. It does not manipulate (intervene in or treat) the weather. Example indicators:

```
CDC = -0.68  (deep pressure trough)
IVD = 0.89   (dense fog warning level)
NP  = 156h   (large stationary front)
```

These indicate "the current weather of society."

Most financial and political crises do not erupt suddenly — they manifest as **an accumulation of pressure drops**. Taking the 2008 financial crisis as an example: a structural divergence existed between the "political narrative (clear skies forecast: the crisis is limited)" and the "market price (a storm pricing in default)." At that point, CDC (pressure difference) was almost certainly showing an extreme value. The crisis was not unpredictable — **it was unobserved**.

## 4. The Weather Radar (Isobar Dynamics) Model

The next stage beyond the barometer is the "weather radar."

The barometer captures a static state; the radar observes **the dynamics of the atmosphere — the waveform of change**.

In GemminAI, the following indicators become critical:

```
ΔCDC / Δt  (isobar density — rate of pressure change)
ΔIVD / Δt  (sudden visibility shift)
ΔFear / Δt (speed of thundercloud development)
```

In other words: **the rate of change in society's weather**.

A meteorologist predicts the arrival of a storm not from absolute wind speed alone, but from **the density of isobars (rate of change)**. Likewise, social crises manifest not as absolute values, but as sudden atmospheric pressure changes — velocity.
