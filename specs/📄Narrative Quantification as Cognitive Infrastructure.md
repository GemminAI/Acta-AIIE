#### **1. Introduction**

Current Artificial Intelligence (LLM) predominantly relies on probabilistic language modeling, often failing to explicitly represent narrative structures or causal linkages. In contrast, human cognition organizes and processes events through **"Narratives"**—structured frameworks of actors, motives, conflicts, and outcomes. This paper defines "Narrative Quantification" as the conversion of these cognitive structures into computable data, serving as the essential cognitive infrastructure for next-generation AI.

#### **2. The Narrative Processing Loop**

Human cognitive processes follow an irreversible loop:

**Event → Meaning → Narrative → Memory**

Through this mechanism, complex experiences are compressed into a format optimized for future decision-making. Narrative cognition is not merely a cultural byproduct but a fundamental biological processing layer for managing environmental complexity.

#### **3. The Narrative Compiler: From Text to Vector**

To bridge the gap between human perception and machine processing, the "Narrative Compiler" extracts structural intent from raw data.

- **Structural Decomposition**: Fragmenting unstructured text into defined nodes (Actor, Intent, Action, Obstacle).
    
- **Vector Fixation**: Assigning mathematical values to these nodes based on the AIIE Protocol, ensuring that the "narrative" is no longer a subjective interpretation but a fixed point in a multi-dimensional coordinate system.
    

#### **4. Conclusion: Anchoring Reality**

By quantifying narratives, we provide AI with a "Contextual Anchor." This prevents the hallucination of intent and allows for the objective observation of how information shapes perceived reality.
